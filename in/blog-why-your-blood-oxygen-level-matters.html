Understanding SpO2: The Basics

SpO2, or peripheral oxygen saturation, represents the percentage of oxygen-saturated hemoglobin in your blood. In simpler terms, it indicates how efficiently your body delivers oxygen to cells and tissuesâ€”a fundamental process for every function in your body.

Healthy adults typically maintain SpO2 levels between 95% and 100% during normal activities and sleep. When levels drop below 90%, a condition called hypoxemia occurs, which can have serious health implications if sustained over time.

The Health Insights Hidden in Your SpO2 Data

Your blood oxygen levels can reveal surprising insights about your overall health:

Sleep Breathing Quality

One of the most valuable applications of continuous SpO2 monitoring is identifying potential sleep-disordered breathing issues. Conditions like sleep apnea cause repeated drops in blood oxygen during sleep, often without the person realizing it. These episodes can lead to:

  • Fragmented, non-restorative sleep
  • Morning headaches
  • Daytime fatigue
  • Increased risk of cardiovascular problems
  • Cognitive impairment

The ZivaOne's ability to track these nighttime fluctuations provides early warning signs that might otherwise go undetected until symptoms become severe.

Respiratory Health Indicators

Your SpO2 trends can also provide insights about your respiratory system's overall function. Subtle changes might indicate:

  • Early signs of respiratory infections
  • Asthma-related breathing challenges
  • Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) management needs
  • Altitude acclimation issues

By establishing your normal baseline, ZivaOne helps you recognize meaningful changes that might warrant attention.

Athletic Performance and Recovery

For active ZivaOne users, SpO2 data offers valuable metrics on:

  • Oxygen utilization efficiency during training
  • Recovery quality after intense exercise
  • Adaptation to altitude training
  • Potential overtraining indicators

Elite athletes have long used SpO2 monitoring to optimize performance; now ZivaOne brings this capability to everyday fitness enthusiasts.
